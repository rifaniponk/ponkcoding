Hourly billing quietly punishes experience. The faster you solve a problem, the less you earn for solving it — which is exactly backwards from how value works.

## Price the outcome, not the hours

A senior engineer's value is compressed time: the same result in a fraction of the hours. Fixed-scope pricing captures that. You agree on a deliverable and a number, and efficiency becomes your margin instead of your penalty.

## Scope is the contract

Fixed price only works with fixed scope. I write the scope in plain language, list what is explicitly out, and treat every change request as a small new agreement. Ambiguity is where fixed-price projects go to lose money.

## The structure that works for me

A discovery phase billed on its own, then a fixed price for the defined build, then an optional retainer for ongoing work. Each phase de-risks the next, and the client always knows what the next number is before they commit to it.
